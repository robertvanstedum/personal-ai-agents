## What is a guild?

The historical guild was a group of practitioners who shared knowledge,
maintained standards, trained each other, and produced work that individual
craftsmen could not produce alone. What died in the industrial age was the
physical co-location requirement.

What the mini-moi-agent-guild rebuilds is the knowledge-sharing and standards
layer — with AI agents as the guild members.

A guild doesn't just execute tasks. It builds and preserves expertise across
engagements, roles, and years. The work compounds. The craft improves. The
knowledge stays yours.

Each member does what they do best.
Robert is the master craftsman. The agents are the guild.

## The charter

**Local-first.** Data stays on your machine. The guild serves you, not a cloud provider.

**Model-agnostic.** No single AI vendor lock-in. The platform works with whatever
models are best for each task.

**Specific intelligence.** The guild knows Robert — his background, his goals, his
ways of working, his domains. Generic AI is everywhere. Specific AI is rare.

**Compounding knowledge.** Every session adds to the corpus. Every domain informs
the others. The platform gets more useful over time, not less.

**Craft discipline.** Artifacts are real — OpenAPI specs, sequence diagrams,
structured documents. Not just conversations. Work product that transfers.

## Ways of working

These are binding across all sessions and all agents:

1. **Claude.ai** designs and produces artifacts. Does not touch git.
2. **OpenClaw** manages file system, memory, and cross-session context. Does not touch git.
3. **Claude Code** handles all git operations — commit, push, branch, merge. Nothing else does.
4. **Robert** is the decision point between agents. His call is final.
5. Design happens before implementation. Artifacts before code.
6. No company-specific IP in the personal repo. Patterns are generic. Standards are public.
