## Core Framework: Hegemonic Transition as Systemic Process

China's trajectory involves navigating — and potentially reconfiguring — that dependency structure, not simply graduating from it.

## Geopolitical Theory as Political Instrument

Where Arrighi's framework suggests China could be the next hegemon in a recurring pattern, Kotkin (via the CCP's own anxieties) points to *discontinuity* — the possibility that Leninist party-states face specific pathologies that disrupt smooth hegemonic succession.

Soviet collapse suggests ideological rigidity can derail that process. The CCP's obsessive study of Soviet failure indicates awareness of this vulnerability, but it's unclear whether their diagnosis is correct or their remedies sufficient.
