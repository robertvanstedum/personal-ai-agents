1. Why This Matters

The Supreme Court striking down Trump's tariffs as unconstitutional creates immediate fiscal pressure given significant tariff revenue collection in recent years, potentially forcing refunds that would worsen federal deficits. This fundamentally challenges executive authority over trade policy and forces either Congressional action (requiring potential filibuster reform) or novel administrative workarounds that could face further legal challenges.

2. Core Argument

The Court ruled Trump's tariff implementation violated constitutional procedures, though the article doesn't specify the exact constitutional defect (likely related to delegation of congressional taxing authority or improper use of executive emergency powers). The administration claims backup implementation options exist, suggesting alternative statutory authorities or procedural modifications.
