### 1. Why This Matters
Friedman argues European outrage at Trump/Vance rhetoric masks a deeper structural reality: Europe lacks alternatives to the US alliance despite political theater. This reframes transatlantic tensions as noise rather than signal—Europe's fragmentation and dependence mean complaints don't translate to actual strategic pivot, which has major implications for interpreting alliance durability under stress.

### 2. Core Argument
Europe's offense at US behavior (Vance's Munich speech criticisms) is irrelevant to alliance persistence because: (1) European nations have irreconcilable internal interests preventing unified action, (2) no credible alternative security guarantor exists (Russia is threat, China geographically distant), and (3) individual European states lack capacity to defend themselves independently. Emotional reactions ≠ strategic options.

### 3. Contrarian Take
- Critics argue Friedman underestimates European agency—past decades show EU can create common institutions (euro, common market) when existentially motivated; Trump-era could accelerate defense integration
- Ignores possibility of European accommodation with Russia or China as hedge strategy, even if not full replacement of US alliance

### 4. Connections

Echoes realist IR theory emphasizing structural constraints over diplomatic rhetoric. Reflects broader populist-era disconnect between public outrage and geopolitical fundamentals.
